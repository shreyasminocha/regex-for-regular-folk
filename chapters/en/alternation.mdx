---
title: Alternation
---

Alternation allows matching _one_ of several phrases. This is more powerful than character classes, which are limited to characters.

Delimit the set of phrases with pipes—`|`.

<Example regex={/foo|bar|baz/g}>
    <li>foo baz</li>
    <li>Your food</li>
    <li>Behind bars</li>
</Example>

> One of `foo`, `bar`, and `baz`

---

If only a part of the regex is to be "alternated", wrap that part with a [group](/chapter/groups)—capturing or non-capturing.

<Example regex={/Try (foo|bar|baz)/g}>
    <li>Try foo</li>
    <li>Try bar</li>
    <li>Try baz</li>
    <li>Try food</li>
</Example>

> `Try` followed by one of `foo`, `bar`, and `baz`

---

Matching numbers between 100 and 250:

<Example regex={/1\d\d|2[0-4]\d|250/g}>
    <li>100, 157, 199</li>
    <li>139 + 140 = 279</li>
    <li>201 INR</li>
    <li>$220</li>
    <li>250</li>
    <li>1250</li>
    <li>e = 2.71828182...</li>
    <li>251</li>
    <li>729</li>
</Example>

This can be generalized to [match arbitrary number ranges](http://gamon.webfactional.com/regexnumericrangegenerator)!

## Examples

### Hex colours

Let's improve one of our older examples to also match shorthand hex colours.

<Example regex={/#([0-9A-F]{6}|[0-9A-F]{3})/g}>
    <li>#AE25AE</li>
    <li>#663399</li>
    <li>How about #73FA79?</li>
    <li>Part of #73FA79BAC too</li>
    <li>#FFF</li>
    <li>#a2ca2c</li>
</Example>

It is important that `[0-9A-F]{6}` comes before `[0-9A-F]{3}`. Else:

<Example regex={/#([0-9A-F]{3}|[0-9A-F]{6})/g}>
    <li>#AE25AE</li>
    <li>#663399</li>
    <li>How about #73FA79?</li>
    <li>Part of #73FA79BAC too</li>
    <li>#FFF</li>
    <li>#a2ca2c</li>
</Example>

<Tip>Regex engines try alternatives from the left to the right.</Tip>

### Roman numerals

<Example regex={/^M{0,4}(CM|CD|D?C{0,3})(XC|XL|L?X{0,3})(IX|IV|V?I{0,3})$/g}>
    <li>MMXX</li>
    <li>VI</li>
    <li>XX</li>
    <li>XI</li>
    <li>IXI</li>
    <li>VV</li>
</Example>
